# Licensing Model

Last Updated: 2026-03-23

## Overview
IBeam uses an open-core model:
- Core framework: Apache License 2.0
- Enterprise add-ons and premium offerings: Commercial license

This approach keeps the core open and forkable for everyone while allowing sustainable enterprise monetization.

## What Is Open Source
The source code in the core framework is licensed under Apache-2.0.

You can:
- use it commercially,
- modify it,
- self-host it,
- fork it,
- redistribute it under Apache-2.0 terms.

## What May Be Commercial
Some optional modules may be marked as commercial add-ons.
These are intended for enterprise scenarios such as:
- advanced compliance and governance packages,
- premium connectors,
- operational tooling and paid support bundles.

Commercial artifacts will always be clearly identified.

## Audience and Pricing Intent
IBeam is intentionally builder-friendly.

Current policy intent:
- Individuals and small teams: free use of core, and potentially free or discounted access to selected commercial add-ons.
- Enterprise organizations: paid commercial terms for enterprise add-ons.

### Draft Threshold Guidance (Subject to Final Pricing)
- Free/small-team tier candidate: under 25 employees OR under USD 5M annual revenue.
- Enterprise commercial tier candidate: 25+ employees OR USD 5M+ annual revenue.

These thresholds are placeholders and may change with public pricing publication.

## Forking and Contributions
- Forks of the Apache-2.0 core are welcome.
- Contributions to core are accepted under the repository contribution process.
- Commercial add-on repositories may have additional contribution rules.
